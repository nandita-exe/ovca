FAQ

Ovarian cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the ovaries. It is often diagnosed at an advanced stage, making treatment more challenging. Early detection is crucial for improving survival rates.

Machine learning uses algorithms to analyze data from a diverse group of women, including information about their medical history and various biomarkers. By identifying patterns and relationships in the data, machine learning models can predict the likelihood of ovarian cancer with high accuracy.

Biomarkers are substances or molecules that can be measured in the blood or other bodily fluids and can indicate the presence of cancer. Examples include specific proteins and genetic markers that may be associated with ovarian cancer.

Our machine learning model has achieved a remarkable 91% accuracy in predicting ovarian cancer. This represents a significant improvement in early detection and offers women a better chance at successful treatment and survival.

Our ongoing research focuses on validating and refining the model further to improve its accuracy and reliability. We aim to continue contributing to better health outcomes for women worldwide.
